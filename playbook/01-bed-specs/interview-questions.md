# Interview Questions — Bed Specs & Build Days

Answer these however is easiest (written, voice memo, call transcript). The
chapter gets drafted from the answers, then reviewed before the TODO banner
comes off.

1. What are the exact dimensions of each frame type (twin, toddler, bunk)? Do drawings or plans exist anywhere (paper, photos, someone's head)?
2. Walk me through the cut list for one twin frame: every board, its dimensions, and the lumber you buy it from.
3. What tools does a build day need, and how many volunteers can work at once per workstation?
4. How long does one frame take, and how many frames does a typical build day produce with how many people?
5. What finish do you apply (stain, paint, sealant)? Any drying-time logistics?
6. Where do the plaques come from and how are they personalized/attached?
7. What safety rules are non-negotiable on a build day? Any injuries or near-misses that shaped them?
8. What does the quality check before a frame ships look like? What gets a frame rejected?
9. The constructed-frame checklist in the system lists Headboard, Footboard, Left/Right Side Rail, Slats, Bolts/Hardware — anything else that travels with a frame?

---

*Answers can be partial — a chapter can ship at 80% and improve when a new
city asks the question you skipped.*
