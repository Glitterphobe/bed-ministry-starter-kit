# Data Care — Protecting the Families You Serve

*Bed Ministry Starter Kit — read this before your system holds its first real request.*

Your workbook holds names, phone numbers, and home addresses of families in
crisis — including their children's ages and genders. Treat it like a locked
filing cabinet of confidential church records, because that is what it is.

## Who can see what

- **The workbook (spreadsheet):** share with **named, trusted volunteers
  only**, as Editors, via the Share button. Never set it to "Anyone with
  the link." Review the share list a few times a year and remove volunteers
  who have moved on — this is the single most important habit on this page.
- **The form link:** safe to share freely. People with the form link can
  only *submit* a request; they cannot see anyone else's data.
- **Own the system from a ministry account,** not someone's personal Gmail.
  Record who holds the password and name a successor — ministries outlive
  their founders' laptops.

## What never leaves the workbook

- **Never post a screenshot of the Waiting List** — or any tab with names —
  in a newsletter, sermon slide, social media post, or group chat. For
  shareable numbers (families served, beds delivered), use the **Dashboard**
  tab: totals only, no names, and it exists exactly for this.
- **Printed pick lists carry a family's name and address.** Collect them
  after the delivery and shred them — don't leave them in vehicles or
  storage rooms.
- **Alert emails** (the addresses you entered in the setup wizard) can
  contain request details. Ministry leads' addresses only.
- Texting a delivery team an address is part of the ministry; forwarding
  spreadsheets or exports is not. If a report is needed, build it from the
  Dashboard totals.

## Housekeeping

- **Departing volunteer or lost device:** remove their access (Share →
  remove) the same day. If they were the ministry account itself, change
  that password.
- **Retention:** *Completed Deliveries* is your history and feeds your
  totals. If your church has a records-retention policy for benevolence
  data, apply it here too; deleting rows is permanent.
- **Rules may apply to you.** Some places regulate child or privacy data,
  and referral partners (foster-care agencies) often add requirements. A
  ten-minute chat with whoever advises your church on legal matters is
  worth having.

*One sentence to give every volunteer: "Family information stays in the
system — we never screenshot it, copy it out, or talk about it beyond
what's needed to deliver the beds."*
